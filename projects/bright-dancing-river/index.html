    A Brief History

    1950

    The First Championship

    The inaugural Formula 1 World Championship kicked off at Silverstone. Giuseppe Farina took the first ever title for Alfa Romeo.

    1977

    Ground Effect Era

    Lotus introduced ground-effect aerodynamics with the Lotus 78, revolutionizing how F1 cars generated downforce.

    1994

    Safety Revolution

    After a tragic San Marino weekend, F1 underwent sweeping safety reforms that continue to shape the sport today.

    2014

    Hybrid Era Begins

    F1 transitioned to 1.6L V6 turbo-hybrid power units — the most thermally efficient race engines ever built.

    2022

    New Aero Regulations

    Cars returned to ground-effect floors, designed to allow closer racing and reduce dirty air for following cars.

    2026

    Power Unit Reset

    New PU regulations bring a 50/50 split between combustion and electric power, plus fully sustainable fuels.
